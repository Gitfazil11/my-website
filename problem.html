Identifying The Problem

Specific Problems Human-Elephant Conflict: Roads cutting through elephant habitats lead to increased human-elephant conflict, with elephants entering farmlands for food and destroying crops. Habitat Fragmentation: Roads and other development projects fragment elephant habitats, disrupting their natural movements and increasing their proximity to human settlements. Risk to Drivers and Elephants: When elephants cross roads, they can be a hazard to drivers, leading to accidents that injure or kill both people and elephants. Elephant Aggression: Male elephants in a state of "musth" (a period of heightened aggression) are particularly dangerous and should be avoided at all costs.
